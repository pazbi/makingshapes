Making shapes is the collaborative project of Hannah and Evgeniy. They are excited to bring together their complementary skills and interests to create something truly magical.

Hannah Wardle is an architectural lighting designer, with an MSc in Light & Lighting from the Bartlett UCL and a BA in Art Textiles from Goldsmiths College UCL. She founded Drws y Coed (Door to the Woods) and has completed installations for a wide variety of clients including Ruthin Craft Centre, Mostyn Gallery and Oriel Myrddin, she has exhibited at 100% Design and Tent as part of London Design Festival.

Evgeniy Kazannik is a photographer and visual artist publishing with The Guardian, Time Out and The Wire among many others. His work is preoccupied with the way light affects our perception of space and in turn affects our state of mind. He is a founder of Green Lens Studios, London's first sustainable photography studio and events space. Yev has experience as a lighting designer, set builder and maker.

A combined fascination with geometry, patterns and light have led Hannah and Yev to design together. With more than 20 years of shared experience working on commercial and artistic commissions they have the capability to create a multitude of unique, sensitive and memorable spaces by introducing their atmospheric and playful light sculptures into any space both indoor and out of doors.
